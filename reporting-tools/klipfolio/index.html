What Klipfolio Actually Does (and the Two-Product Reality)

Here's what's genuinely interesting about Klipfolio: the company started in 2001 as a consumer app. Wille and Matthews were building personal dashboards — widgets that pulled live data from websites so you didn't have to keep refreshing tabs manually. Then Lufthansa called, because a lot of their employees were using Klipfolio to track soccer scores. That random enterprise customer turned into a pivot to business intelligence, and Klipfolio has been making B2B dashboards ever since. It's a weird founding story, and the company has worn that weirdness as a badge.

The current product situation is where things get complicated. Klipfolio ships two distinct products, and the difference between them isn't obvious until you've spent time inside both.

Klips is the original product — a dashboard builder that connects to 130+ data sources, lets you pull and transform data with Excel-like formulas, and render it in highly customizable visualizations. It's powerful, it's flexible, and it requires real setup work. Each "Klip" is essentially a configurable data widget; you build your dashboard by assembling Klips, configuring each one against a data source, and then arranging them in a layout. Think of it as the toolkit for people who want to build exactly the dashboard they have in their head, not pick from a template.

PowerMetrics is the newer product — a metric-centric analytics platform designed for more accessible self-serve analysis. Instead of starting with data and building visualizations, you start by defining metrics that your whole team agrees on, then explore and share them. PowerMetrics has a free plan, AI-powered natural language querying, and a more modern UI. It's aimed at the use case where your team needs shared, trusted metrics rather than a bespoke visualization layer.

The honest reaction from most Capterra reviewers who use both: "The difference between Klips and PowerMetrics is not immediately apparent — it's a lot of trial and error to figure out how to maximize each." That's not a knock on the product team; it's a real architectural tension. Two products serving overlapping use cases is something Klipfolio will need to address clearly as it competes with tools that offer a single coherent product story.

Features That Actually Matter

Klips: Deep Customization With Real Formula Power

The Klips formula engine is one of the genuine differentiators. You can pull data from a source, then transform it with something close to spreadsheet logic — date manipulation, conditional formatting, cross-source calculations, string operations. If your data comes in messily formatted (and it always does), Klips lets you clean and reshape it inside the platform rather than preprocessing it externally.

The chart library covers 30+ visualization types, and the layout system gives you pixel-level control over how things appear. This matters for agencies whose clients have specific brand requirements, and for teams displaying dashboards on large screens where layout precision actually shows up. Light mode and dark mode are both supported and look polished. PDF export, scheduled email delivery, and CSV exports are all available.

130+ Data Connections: Broad Coverage With a REST API Escape Hatch

The connection library covers major marketing platforms — Google Ads, GA4, Facebook Ads, LinkedIn, HubSpot, Salesforce — plus SQL databases, REST APIs, spreadsheets, and data warehouses. The REST API connector is particularly useful: it means Klipfolio can connect to almost anything that has an API, even if there's no pre-built connector. This is meaningful for teams with non-standard data sources or internal systems that don't appear on anyone's integration list.

The downside of the REST API approach: it requires you to know how to configure it. Adding a common data source like HubSpot or GA4 is straightforward. Building a custom REST connection to an internal CRM or a niche platform takes technical work. That pattern — easy for mainstream connections, harder for custom ones — runs through most of Klipfolio's product.

White-Labeling: A Genuine Agency Differentiator

Klipfolio's white-labeling is one of the better implementations in this price range. Agencies can brand dashboards with client logos, apply custom themes, and configure the login experience so clients see the agency's brand rather than Klipfolio's. This appears at lower price points than comparable features in some competitors — making it accessible for agencies that can't absorb premium pricing just for branding.

One caveat: full white-labeling, custom themes, and SSO require the Pro tier at $420/month or the Premier tier at $690/month. The lower Klips plans support some customization but not the full branded client experience. Agencies building a white-labeled dashboard product for multiple clients should budget for Pro or Premier, not the entry-level plans.

PowerMetrics AI: Natural Language Querying

PowerMetrics includes AI-powered querying — you can ask questions about your data in natural language and get visualized answers without configuring a chart manually. "Show me our top 5 campaigns by revenue last quarter" produces a chart; you don't have to build it. This is genuinely useful for self-serve analytics and for team members who aren't data-fluent enough to build their own Klips.

Where Klipfolio Falls Short

The Learning Curve Is Real (Especially for Non-Technical Users)

This is the single most consistent complaint across G2, Capterra, and the handful of Reddit threads I've dug through. Building custom Klips takes time. A simple Klip connecting a standard metric from a pre-built connector might take 5–10 minutes. A more complex Klip with custom calculations, multi-source blending, or non-standard data can take an hour or more. Add the need for multiple Klips per dashboard, and you're talking about a meaningful setup investment before you have anything to show a client.

The formula syntax, while powerful, has a learning curve of its own. One agency reviewer on Capterra described it honestly: "Creating the backend of the dashboard is very daunting for someone who isn't very familiar with large datasets and coding. I've spent countless hours watching YouTube tutorials learning how to build out Klips." They paid a developer to create API connections — and those connections needed updating regularly. That's not a failed product; it's a clear signal about who the product is actually for.

"Building any custom visualizations or connecting any data source basically requires a developer — no kidding."
Verified G2 reviewer — Klipfolio user, 2024

Actually, I want to push back on myself slightly here. The learning curve complaint is real, but it's not universal. The same reviewers who complain about complexity for custom builds consistently praise how easy standard connections are. Adding GA4, Google Ads, or HubSpot is described as quick and straightforward. The difficulty kicks in when you're trying to do something genuinely custom — which, depending on your use case, may never happen.

Performance on Complex Dashboards

Several G2 reviewers, including one named Graham H., specifically called out slow load times on more complex dashboards. "Klipfolio can be slow, particularly when performing more complex tasks — even less advanced reports with basic visualization can take a long time to load." Latency is a real concern when dashboards are displayed on TV screens or shared with clients who have no patience for spinning load states.

This seems to be most pronounced on dashboards with many data sources pulling simultaneously, or on plans with less frequent refresh rates. Whether it's a fundamental architecture issue or a plan-tier limitation is something each team will need to test for their specific setup.

Customer Support Is Tier-Gated

On lower Klips plans, support is limited to email and self-service documentation. Live chat and prioritized support require the Pro plan ($420/month). At $690/month (Premier), you get dedicated onboarding support and account management. For teams getting stuck on Klips configuration — which happens — waiting for email support instead of getting a live chat answer is a friction that affects time-to-value.

The documentation is reasonably comprehensive, and the community is helpful, but "check the docs" isn't a satisfying answer when you're trying to build a specific dashboard for a client review tomorrow.

Two Products, One Confusing Product Story

This is less a complaint about capability and more about buyer experience. When you're evaluating Klipfolio, you need to understand which product you're actually buying, because Klips and PowerMetrics have different pricing, different UIs, and different use cases. The fact that they share a brand and some infrastructure doesn't mean the decision is obvious. Several Capterra reviewers explicitly noted that even after buying and using both, the distinction between the two wasn't clear without significant trial and error.

Who Should (and Shouldn't) Use Klipfolio

It's a strong fit if:

  • You have technical resources — a data analyst or developer — who can own the Klips configuration
  • You need pixel-perfect custom dashboards that match client branding precisely
  • Your data sources include non-standard systems that need REST API connections
  • White-labeling is a business requirement and you can't absorb Whatagraph's pricing
  • You want dashboard software built on 25 years of iteration, not a venture-backed startup

It's probably not the right tool if:

  • Your team is non-technical and needs dashboards running within a day — try Databox
  • You need automated client report delivery with scheduling — Whatagraph handles this better
  • Performance on heavy dashboards is critical — test your specific use case carefully before committing
  • You want AI summaries without paying $300+/month — alternatives include this earlier in their plans
  • Budget is limited and you want the cheapest entry into white-labeling — Klips Pro at $420/month is the minimum for a real branded experience

Whether they'll eventually unify the two products or continue running parallel tracks is the interesting strategic question. For buyers, the practical implication is that PowerMetrics is where new features are landing — if you're evaluating Klipfolio today and AI-powered analysis matters to you, PowerMetrics is where to start.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does Klipfolio cost in 2026?
Two products with separate pricing. Klips starts at ~$80/month (annual). Full white-label and priority support require Pro at ~$420/month. PowerMetrics has a Free plan (2 editors, 2 dashboards), Go at $125/month, Pro at $300/month, and Business at $800/month. Annual billing applies to all paid plans; monthly billing is available at higher rates.
What's the difference between Klips and PowerMetrics?
Klips is the original product — a highly flexible dashboard builder with Excel-like formulas, 30+ chart types, and 130+ data connections. Great for custom, pixel-perfect dashboards. PowerMetrics is newer — a metric-centric analytics platform with AI querying, standardized metric management, and a more accessible UI. Klips gives you maximum control; PowerMetrics is faster to adopt. The right choice depends on whether you value customization or accessibility more.
Is Klipfolio good for agencies?
It can work well for agencies — white-labeling, 130+ connections, and PDF/email delivery are all there. The challenge is setup complexity: building Klips takes real investment, and the full branded experience requires Pro ($420/month). Agencies that need faster setup and automated client delivery should look at Whatagraph, which is purpose-built for that workflow.
Does Klipfolio have a free plan?
PowerMetrics has a Free plan with 2 editor users and 2 dashboards. Klips has a 14-day free trial (no credit card required). The PowerMetrics free plan is a good way to explore the metric management use case; the Klips trial gives you full access to the dashboard builder for two weeks.
How does Klipfolio compare to Databox?
Klipfolio is more customizable — the formula engine and chart options give you more control over what dashboards look like. Databox is faster to set up (one-click OAuth, templates), has a better mobile experience, and includes goals and forecasting natively. For teams that need custom-built dashboards and have technical resources, Klipfolio wins. For teams wanting to be up and running in an afternoon, Databox is easier.
What are the biggest complaints about Klipfolio?
The recurring themes: (1) steep learning curve for custom Klips builds — non-technical users often need developer help for complex visualizations; (2) slow load times on dashboards with many simultaneous data sources; (3) support is limited to email on lower plans, which creates friction when stuck on configuration; (4) the difference between Klips and PowerMetrics is confusing and requires trial-and-error to navigate.

The Bottom Line

Klipfolio is one of the few tools in this category that genuinely rewards the time you put into it. If you have technical resources and a clear vision for what your dashboards need to look like, the Klips formula engine and visualization flexibility give you control that most competitors don't match at this price point. The 25 years of iteration shows up in product depth that newer tools simply haven't had time to build.

That said, I can't recommend it as a casual first choice for teams without data-literate staff. The learning curve is real, the two-product structure adds decision overhead, and the support experience on lower plans leaves you on your own when things get complicated. One Capterra reviewer put it plainly: "Endless capabilities, but since we pay for a lower price service we are really left on our own to figure out the back-end." That trade-off — maximum capability in exchange for owning the setup — is what Klipfolio has always asked of its users.

My honest recommendation: if white-labeled custom dashboards are a real business need and you have someone technical to own the build, Klipfolio Pro is worth serious consideration. Try the 14-day Klips trial specifically on the connector and formula workflow you actually need — not a generic demo. If the complexity feels manageable in two weeks, you'll know you have the right fit. If you're still watching YouTube tutorials at day 13, Databox or Whatagraph will save you the headache.
